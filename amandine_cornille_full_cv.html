TEACHING and OUTREACH ACTIVITIES

I have accumulated over 300 hours of teaching experience during my PhD and postdoctoral positions. Between 2018 and 2024, I taught approximately 25 hours per year, including field-based and outreach activities. Since 2024, I have taught undergraduate courses at NYU Abu Dhabi, including Evolution (BIOL-UH 2113), Diversity (CDAD-UH 1010EQ), and Population Genomics (BIOL-UH-3125-AD-001). My teaching integrates evolutionary biology, genomics, and biodiversity, often linking research with field-based learning and public science communication.

STUDENT SUPERVISION

Since 2018, I have supervised over 47 students, including PhD candidates, MSc and BSc students, as well as short-term interns. Most of them have published with me or are currently preparing manuscripts. This includes students from French institutions as well as NYU Abu Dhabi, such as Ronan Dadole, Maxime Criado, Yuqi Nie, Nancy Jing, Aru Zhamalbek, Sadaf Habib, and Sanzhar Kakenov. My supervision has focused on evolutionary genomics, domestication, and adaptation in fruit trees and their associated organisms.
